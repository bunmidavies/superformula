About the superformula

The superformula was made/described by Johan Gielis in 2003, and it is a formula that can be used to describe many lots of different shapes and curves, mainly the kind of shapes found in nature i.e. trees or bushes etc.

The superformula is written in polar co-ordinates. Polar co-ordinates are different to the usual cartesian co-ordinates which are (x,y), as polar co-ordinates have (r,ϴ), where r is the distance from the origin to the point, and ϴ is the angle (in radians), from the x axis.

The complete superformula is:

n1, n2, n3 and m can be any number, but a and b can be anything but 0 (since you can't divide by 0).

A bigger m = more rotational symmetry

When the n's are lower but the same the form will become pinched

Polygonal shapes can be made with high values of n1, and the high/equal values for n2 and n3

Starfish shapes can be made with smaller values of n1 than n2 and n3

In the demo, the cy is the number of the times the loop repeats * pi
